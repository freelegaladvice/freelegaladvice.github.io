Avant Loans for Students: Are They an Option?

  • Home
  • Blog
  • Blog Details

In today’s fast-paced world, higher education is more expensive than ever. With tuition fees skyrocketing and living costs increasing, many students find themselves in a financial bind. Traditional student loans, scholarships, and grants may not always cover the full cost of education, leaving students searching for alternative funding options. One such option is Avant Loans—a personal loan provider that has gained attention for its flexible terms and quick approval process. But are Avant Loans a viable choice for students? Let’s dive deep into this topic.

Understanding Avant Loans

What Are Avant Loans?

Avant is an online lending platform that specializes in personal loans ranging from $2,000 to $35,000. Unlike federal student loans, Avant Loans are unsecured, meaning they don’t require collateral. They are designed for borrowers with fair to good credit scores (typically 600 and above) and offer fixed interest rates with repayment terms of 24 to 60 months.

How Do They Work?

Avant Loans are primarily used for debt consolidation, home improvements, or emergency expenses. While they aren’t marketed specifically for education, some students have considered them as an alternative to cover gaps in funding. The application process is entirely online, with funds often deposited as soon as the next business day after approval.

Pros and Cons of Avant Loans for Students

Advantages

1. Fast Funding

Unlike federal student loans, which can take weeks to process, Avant Loans provide quick access to cash. This can be crucial for students facing unexpected expenses like textbooks, housing deposits, or last-minute tuition fees.

2. No Collateral Required

Since Avant Loans are unsecured, students don’t need to put up assets like a car or house as security. This makes them less risky compared to secured loans.

3. Flexible Use of Funds

Federal student loans often come with restrictions on how the money can be spent. Avant Loans, however, can be used for any purpose, giving students more financial flexibility.

Disadvantages

1. Higher Interest Rates

Avant Loans typically have higher APRs (ranging from 9.95% to 35.99%) compared to federal student loans, which often have lower fixed rates. This means students could end up paying significantly more over time.

2. Credit Score Requirements

Federal student loans don’t require a credit check (for most borrowers), but Avant Loans do. Students with limited or poor credit history may struggle to qualify or may only be offered high-interest rates.

3. No Student Loan Benefits

Federal loans come with perks like income-driven repayment plans, loan forgiveness programs, and deferment options. Avant Loans lack these protections, making them a riskier long-term choice.

Comparing Avant Loans to Other Student Financing Options

Federal Student Loans

Federal loans remain the gold standard for student financing due to their low fixed rates and borrower protections. However, they have borrowing limits, which may not cover all expenses.

Private Student Loans

Private lenders like Sallie Mae or Discover offer loans specifically for education, often with competitive rates for well-qualified borrowers. However, they also require credit checks and may not offer the same flexibility as Avant Loans in terms of fund usage.

Credit Cards

Some students resort to credit cards for emergencies, but these often come with even higher interest rates (sometimes exceeding 20%). Avant Loans, while not ideal, may still be a better option than racking up credit card debt.

When Might an Avant Loan Make Sense for Students?

Scenario 1: Emergency Expenses

If a student faces an unexpected cost—like a broken laptop needed for coursework—and doesn’t qualify for additional federal aid, an Avant Loan could provide quick relief.

Scenario 2: Short-Term Gap Funding

For students who only need a small amount to cover a semester’s expenses and plan to repay it quickly, Avant’s shorter loan terms might be manageable.

Scenario 3: Students with Strong Credit

Those with good credit scores may secure a reasonable APR, making an Avant Loan a more affordable alternative to high-interest credit cards.

Alternatives to Avant Loans for Students

1. Scholarships and Grants

Free money should always be the first option. Websites like Fastweb and Scholarships.com can help students find opportunities they qualify for.

2. Part-Time Work or Side Hustles

Gig economy jobs (Uber, DoorDash, freelancing) can provide extra income without taking on debt.

3. Federal Parent PLUS Loans

If a student’s parents have good credit, they may qualify for a Parent PLUS Loan, which often has better terms than private loans.

4. Crowdfunding

Platforms like GoFundMe allow students to raise money for education from friends, family, and even strangers.

Final Thoughts: Is an Avant Loan Right for You?

While Avant Loans offer speed and flexibility, they come with significant drawbacks, especially for students. Before considering one, explore all federal aid options, scholarships, and part-time work. If an Avant Loan is the only viable choice, ensure you fully understand the repayment terms and have a plan to pay it off quickly to avoid high interest costs.

For students in a financial pinch, Avant Loans might provide a temporary solution—but they should never be the first or only option considered. Always weigh the risks and explore every alternative before committing to any form of debt.
